# Task 4: Churn Rates by Gender

## Objective

The objective of this task is to compare customer churn rates across genders using a Pivot Table and a Clustered Column Chart.

---

## Activities Performed

### 1. Pivot Table Analysis

* Created a Pivot Table to calculate churn counts for each gender.
* Calculated the total number of churned and non-churned customers.
* Computed churn rates for male and female customers.

### 2. Data Visualization

* Created a Clustered Column Chart using Pivot Table data.
* Applied distinct colors for churned and non-churned customers.
* Added chart title, axis labels, and data labels.

### 3. Business Interpretation

* Compared churn behavior across genders.
* Evaluated whether gender significantly influences customer churn.

---

## Churn Statistics

| Gender | Non-Churned | Churned | Total Customers | Churn Rate |
| ------ | ----------- | ------- | --------------- | ---------- |
| Female | 2549        | 939     | 3488            | 26.92%     |
| Male   | 2625        | 930     | 3555            | 26.16%     |

---

## Key Insights

* Female customers have a churn rate of 26.92%.
* Male customers have a churn rate of 26.16%.
* The difference in churn rates between genders is only 0.76%.
* Gender does not appear to significantly influence customer churn.
* Other factors such as contract type, monthly charges, payment methods, and service quality may have a stronger impact on customer retention.

---

## Business Question

If gender has little impact on churn, which factors contribute most to customer attrition?

---

## Conclusion

The analysis indicates that customer churn rates are very similar across genders. Therefore, gender is unlikely to be a primary driver of churn. Further investigation should focus on pricing, contract types, customer services, and payment methods to better understand customer attrition.
