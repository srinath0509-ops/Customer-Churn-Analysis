# Task 5: Heatmap Analysis of Monthly Charges and Tenure

## Objective

The objective of this task is to visualize the interaction between Monthly Charges, Tenure, and Customer Churn using a heatmap and identify churn patterns across different customer segments.

---

## Activities Performed

### 1. Data Preparation

* Created Tenure Groups (0–12, 13–24, 25–36, 37–48, 49–60, 61–72).
* Created Monthly Charge Groups (0–30, 30–60, 60–90, 90–120).

### 2. Pivot Table Analysis

* Created a Pivot Table to calculate churn counts across tenure and monthly charge groups.
* Filtered the data to include churned customers only.

### 3. Heatmap Creation

* Applied Conditional Formatting Color Scales.
* Visualized churn intensity across customer segments.
* Identified high-risk churn groups.

---

---

## Key Insights

* Customers with 0–12 months tenure account for the highest number of churn cases.
* More than half of all churned customers belong to the first year of service.
* The $60–$90 monthly charge range exhibits the highest churn volume.
* Churn gradually decreases as customer tenure increases.
* Long-term customers appear more loyal and less likely to leave the company.
* Customer retention efforts should focus on newly acquired customers, especially those with medium to high monthly charges.

---

## Business Question

How can the company improve retention among customers in their first year of service?

---

## Conclusion

The heatmap reveals a strong relationship between customer tenure and churn behavior. Customers with shorter tenure exhibit significantly higher churn levels, while long-term customers demonstrate greater loyalty. The highest churn concentration is observed among customers with monthly charges between $60 and $90 during their first year of service.
