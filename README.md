# Customer Churn Analysis

An end-to-end Telecom Customer Churn Analysis project developed during my Data Visualization Internship at Saiket Systems.

## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most important business challenges faced by telecommunication companies. The objective of this project is to analyze customer behavior, identify churn patterns, and generate actionable insights that can help improve customer retention.

Using Microsoft Excel, various data analysis and visualization techniques were applied to explore customer demographics, service usage, monthly charges, tenure, and churn behavior.

---

## Dataset Information

* Dataset: Telco Customer Churn Dataset
* Total Records: 7,043
* Total Features: 21
* Domain: Telecommunications
* Target Variable: Customer Churn

## Key Project Findings

### Customer Churn Overview

* Total Churned Customers: 1,869
* Total Active Customers: 5,174
* Churn Rate: 26.53%
* Retention Rate: 73.47%

### Monthly Charges Analysis

* Most customers fall within the $18.25–$28.25 monthly charge range.
* A secondary concentration exists between $78.25–$98.25.
* Monthly charges vary significantly across customer segments.

### Gender Analysis

* Female Churn Rate: 26.92%
* Male Churn Rate: 26.16%
* Gender does not significantly influence customer churn.

### Heatmap Analysis

* Customers with 0–12 months tenure account for the highest churn volume.
* More than half of all churned customers belong to the first year of service.
* Customers paying between $60–$90 monthly charges exhibit the highest churn volume.
* Churn decreases steadily as customer tenure increases.

---

## Business Insights

* New customers are more likely to churn than long-term customers.
* Customer retention efforts should focus on customers during their first year of service.
* Medium to high monthly charge plans require additional attention.
* Gender has minimal impact on churn behavior.
* Tenure and Monthly Charges are stronger indicators of customer churn.
